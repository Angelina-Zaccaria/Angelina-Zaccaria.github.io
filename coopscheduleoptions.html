What is Cooperative Education?

Cooperative education, or "Co-op" is a five year educational program where students alternate between semesters of full-time work and full-time study in areas related to the student's academic major and career goals over the course of their sophomore and junior years.

Benefits of Being a Co-op Student

  • Co-op gives you a chance to apply knowledge and theories gained in the classroom to a 'real-world' environment
  • Co-op work terms allow you to make informed educational and career choices based on full-time, work experiences related to your academic studies and career interests
  • The Co-op program provides resume writing, interview preparation, job search strategies, and ongoing support while students are out on work terms
  • Begin to establish a valuable employer contact network
  • Each co-op assignment is full-time and paid

How do I Join the Co-op Program?

All students majoring in Engineering and Science are invited to attend informational meetings during the first semester of their freshman year. First year students officially join Co-op at the beginning of their second semester.

Co-op Eligibility and Requirements

In order to be eligible for a first work assignment, students must complete all first year course requirements and be in good academic standing.

  • Students must complete one summer of study
  • Students must maintain a minimum GPA of 2.2
  • To maximize your co-op experience, we require one "return" work term at an organization
  • Vacations and time off are not permitted during a co-op assignment

Co-op Work Cycles

Fall: September — December
Spring: January — May
Summer: May — August
